Generalizable Manipulation Skill Learning

Dec. 6-14, NeurIPS 2021 Virtual

Introduction

The study of generalization has played an essential role in many application domains of machine learning, such as image recognition, speech recognition, natural language processing. Surprisingly, in common frameworks of policy learning, e.g., reinforcement learning (RL) and imitation learning (IL), generalizablity has not received the same amount of attention. Only until recently, efforts start to be made to tackle the generalizable policy learning problem. For example, to achieve task-level generalization, meta-RL optimizes fast adaptation in unseen environments; to make the best use of past experiences, offline RL learns an initial policy using previously collected trajectories, and the initial policy may be refined by an online RL process; to improve policy robustness, domain randomization and data augmentation techniques that were originally developed in computer vision are also applied in visual reinforcement/imitation learning.

Manipulation skill learning is an application domain that allows us to explore generalizability along the aforementioned dimensions. For example, a non-generalizable robot manipulation policy can only manipulate the same objects that appeared in the training environments, while a generalizable one would be able to successfully manipulate objects of different colors, geometries, and physical properties unseen during training; it may even be able to handle different numbers of objects at test time (compositional generalizability).

We firmly believe that it is a good timing to start the discussion of generalizable policy learning in NeurIPS. The workshop is designed to foster progress in this topic, in particular, with a focus on manipulation skills because they require complex reasoning involving visual appearance, geometry, and physics. We envision that the workshop will bring together interdisciplinary researchers from machine learning, vision, and robotics to discuss the current and future research of this topic.
